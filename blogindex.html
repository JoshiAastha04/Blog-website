Pav Bhaji

A delicious recipe from West India.

Read More
4 medium potatoes, 1 cup cauliflower, 1/2 cup green peas(boil until soft, mash them together)
Sauté Onions: Heat butter in a pan. Add finely chop onions and sauté until golden
Cook finely chop Tomatoes and
Spices: Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped tomatoes. Cook until soft.
Mix in pav pav bhaji, masala, chili powder, turmeric, and salt.
Add the mashed veggies to the pan and cook, mashing further with the back of a spoon.
Add water as needed for desired consistency.
Let it simmer for 10 minutes. Stir in more butter for extra richness.
Toast Pav: Heat butter on a pan and toast the pav buns until golden. Serve: Garnish the bhaji with lemon juice and coriander. Serve hot with the toasted pav!

Pani Puri

India's Most Fav Street Food

Read More
Prepare Dough for puri: Combine semolina, flour, and water into a stiff dough. Roll into small circles and deep fry until crispy. Set aside.
Make Pani: Blend mint, coriander, green chilies, and ginger into a paste. Mix with cold water, tamarind paste, cumin powder, chaat masala, and salt. Adjust seasoning. Refrigerate.
Prepare Filling: Mix mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chaat masala, and salt.
Assemble: Crack a hole in each puri, stuff with the filling, and dip into the chilled pani. Serve immediately!

Pancakes

Korean Potato Pancakes

Read More
Method : Grate the potatoes and squeeze out excess moisture.
In a bowl, combine grated potatoes, green onions, flour, salt, and pepper.
Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat and add a thin layer of oil.
Scoop a small amount of the mixture into the skillet, flattening it to form pancakes.
Cook for 4-5 minutes on each side until golden brown.
Drain on paper towels and serve warm with dipping sauce (soy sauce or chili sauce).
